# HIVE

## Overview

The **HIVE** is a clandestine, self-sustaining organization that operates as a parallel society, offering its members resources, protection, and a sense of belonging in exchange for loyalty and contribution. Founded approximately 57 years ago by post-war heroes who sheltered civilians from their own leaders, the HIVE has evolved into a complex, hierarchical network with a strict code of conduct and a focus on mutual aid, personal growth, and collective empowerment.

---

## History

- **Origins**: Formed around a group of post-war heroes who chose compassion over violence, hiding civilians from enemy forces and their own corrupt leaders.
- **Evolution**: Initially used army dog tags for identification. As technology advanced, the HIVE adopted tattoos and microchip implants for its members.
- **Legacy**: The original members, now elderly, still participate in the HIVE’s mission, though they have retired from active roles. Their stories are a required part of the initiation process for new members.

---

## Hierarchy and Roles

### Ranks

1. **Guest**: Non-members who are aware of the HIVE’s existence and may attend events. They are not bound by the HIVE’s rules but are expected to respect its values.
2. **Drone**: Full members who have undergone the initiation ritual, which includes receiving a tattoo or microchip implant with a unique ID. Drones must demonstrate loyalty to the HIVE above all else and contribute their skills or resources to the collective.
  - **Requirements**: Loyalty to the HIVE, its members, and its mission. Drones must be willing to serve the HIVE’s needs and prioritize the collective over personal gain.
  - **Benefits**: Access to the HIVE’s vast network of resources, including medical care, education, legal support, and more. Drones can request assistance from the HIVE for almost any need.
3. **Warden**: Enforcers of the HIVE’s rules and values. Wardens oversee drones, ensure compliance with the HIVE’s code, and make judgments on violations.
  - **Responsibilities**: Maintaining order, resolving conflicts, and ensuring the HIVE’s integrity. Wardens also oversee each other to prevent abuse of power.
  - **Authority**: Wardens can command any drone and have access to the HIVE’s central database (Hivemind), which contains records of all members’ successes and failures.
4. **HIVE King/Queen**: A rare, crisis-only role. The HIVE has not had a King or Queen for many years, as the role is reserved for extreme circumstances.

---

## Identification

- **Tattoos**: Drones often have a unique tattoo on their wrist or another discreet location. These tattoos appear as random designs to outsiders but can be scanned by other members to reveal the drone’s profile and history.
- **Microchip Implants**: Some drones opt for a microchip implant, which serves as a permanent, scannable ID. The implant is mandatory for full membership and is inserted during the initiation ritual.
- **Dress Code**: During HIVE events, drones are required to wear specific attire (e.g., latex, leather, or other distinctive outfits) to signify their membership. Outside of events, drones blend into society and can only be identified by their tattoos or implants.

---

## Values and Principles

- **Loyalty**: The cornerstone of the HIVE. Members must demonstrate unwavering loyalty to the HIVE, its mission, and its members. Betrayal is met with severe consequences, including excommunication.
- **Mutual Aid**: The HIVE operates on a system of trade and reciprocity. Members contribute their skills, resources, or labor to the collective, and in return, they receive support and access to the HIVE’s vast network.
- **Accountability**: All members’ actions, successes, and failures are recorded in the Hivemind database. This information is used for internal accountability and to ensure that members uphold the HIVE’s values.
- **Empathy and Compassion**: Members are expected to demonstrate empathy and a willingness to help others, both within and outside the HIVE. Acts of kindness and service are highly valued.
- **Potential Over Parasitism**: The HIVE has no tolerance for members who choose to exploit the system without contributing. Those who refuse to use their potential or act as parasites are either excommunicated or, in extreme cases, reduced to the status of **slaves**—members who must obey the HIVE’s commands without question until they choose to leave.

---

## Initiation Process

- **Testing**: Prospective members are tested on their personality, empathy, and willingness to serve. Tests are not always explicit; the HIVE evaluates a candidate’s character through their actions and interactions in everyday life.
- **Ritual**: The initiation ritual involves receiving a tattoo or microchip implant, symbolizing the member’s permanent commitment to the HIVE. The ritual is held once a year and is a deeply symbolic and transformative experience.
- **Storytelling**: Initiates must learn the stories of the HIVE’s founding members, which are shared during the initiation process. These stories serve as a reminder of the HIVE’s values and the sacrifices made by its earliest members.

---

## Resources and Capabilities

The HIVE’s network includes a wide range of professionals and experts, allowing it to offer almost any service to its members:

- **Medical Care**: Doctors and therapists are available to provide physical and mental health support.
- **Education**: Teachers, lecturers, and scientists offer knowledge and training in various fields.
- **Legal and Financial Support**: Experts provide assistance with legal issues, financial planning, and more.
- **Security**: The HIVE has its own security forces, including guards and a brute force team, to protect its members and enforce its rules.
- **Hackers and Engineers**: Technical experts provide support for digital and physical infrastructure.
- **Shelter and Basic Needs**: The HIVE provides food, shelter, and other necessities to members in need, including homeless individuals who are integrated into the network.

---

## Darker Aspects

- **Slaves**: Members who refuse to contribute or act as parasites may be reduced to the status of slaves. Slaves must obey the HIVE’s commands without question but are free to leave at any time. They are also protected by the HIVE and have access to its resources without the need to repay.
- **Punishments**: Violations of the HIVE’s rules, such as abuse of power or betrayal, are met with severe consequences. Wardens who abuse their authority are stripped of their rank and treated as any other rule-breaking drone.
- **Secrecy**: The HIVE operates in the shadows, and its members are trained to maintain secrecy. Exposure of the HIVE’s operations or members can result in excommunication or worse.

---

## Culture and Community

- **Events**: The HIVE hosts underground events, such as raves, concerts, and gatherings, where members can connect, celebrate, and reinforce their bonds. These events often feature distinctive dress codes and rituals.
- **Support Networks**: The HIVE fosters a strong sense of community, with members supporting each other in times of need. This includes emotional support, practical assistance, and a shared commitment to growth.
- **Influence**: The HIVE’s reach extends beyond its events. Many drones are embedded in mainstream society, operating as professionals, artists, or everyday citizens while maintaining their allegiance to the HIVE.

---

## Notable Practices

- **Excommunication**: Members who violate the HIVE’s rules or betray its values are excommunicated. Their names and faces are displayed on electronic boards at HIVE events, and they are banned from all future gatherings.
- **Hivemind Database**: A central system that records the successes and failures of all members. This database is accessible to wardens and is used to ensure accountability and transparency within the organization.
- **Symbolism**: The HIVE uses symbols, such as the drone helmet, to represent its identity and values. Business cards with the HIVE’s address and symbols are given to prospective members as an invitation to learn more.

---

## Joining the HIVE

- **Invitation**: Prospective members are typically invited by a current member, often a warden. They are given a business card with the HIVE’s address and symbols as a sign of interest.
- **Evaluation**: Candidates are evaluated based on their actions, empathy, and willingness to contribute. There is no formal application process; instead, the HIVE observes the candidate’s behavior and interactions over time.
- **Initiation**: If deemed worthy, the candidate is invited to participate in the initiation ritual, where they receive their tattoo or implant and officially become a drone.
